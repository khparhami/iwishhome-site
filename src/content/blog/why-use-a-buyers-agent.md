Buying a home is likely the single largest financial decision you will ever make. Yet most buyers approach the property market armed with nothing more than a few weekends of open-house visits and a mortgage pre-approval. Meanwhile, the vendor's agent — who is sitting across the negotiating table — is a seasoned professional whose sole job is to extract the highest possible price for their client.

That asymmetry is exactly why a buyer's agent exists.

## What Does a Buyer's Agent Actually Do?

A buyer's agent (also called a buyer's advocate) represents **you** — the purchaser — exclusively. Unlike a selling agent, whose commission is paid by the vendor, your buyer's agent's loyalty is entirely in your corner.

Their services typically include:

- **Property search** — Access to both on-market and off-market listings, saving you months of scrolling
- **Due diligence** — Thorough research on comparable sales, council overlays, flood maps, and building reports
- **Negotiation** — Professional bargaining informed by real market data, not emotion
- **Auction bidding** — Calm, strategic bidding on your behalf when competition is fierce
- **Settlement support** — Coordination between solicitors, conveyancers, and inspectors

## The Cost of Going It Alone

A study by CoreLogic found that Australian buyers who purchased at auction paid on average **5–11% more** than properties transacted via private treaty. Emotional bidding, fear of missing out, and a lack of comparable sale data are the main culprits.

A skilled buyer's agent can often negotiate savings that more than cover their fee — and the peace of mind is priceless.

## Off-Market Access Changes Everything

Some of the best properties in any suburb are sold before they ever hit Domain or realestate.com.au. Buyer's agents with strong local networks hear about these listings first. If you are searching only on the major portals, you are seeing a fraction of the available market.

## Is a Buyer's Agent Right for You?

You would benefit most from working with a buyer's agent if you:

- Are time-poor and cannot attend multiple inspections each weekend
- Are buying in an unfamiliar suburb or interstate
- Have lost out at auction multiple times
- Want access to off-market properties
- Simply want professional guidance through one of life's most significant purchases
