Every Saturday evening, news outlets across Australia report the weekend's auction clearance rate as though it is the definitive measure of the property market's health. A rate above 70% is treated as a bull market; anything below 60% signals softening conditions.

But clearance rates are more nuanced than that — and smart buyers need to understand them properly.

## How Clearance Rates Are Calculated

A clearance rate is simply the percentage of properties that sold (at or before auction) out of those that were scheduled to go to auction. A result of 73% means that of all properties listed for auction that week, 73 in every 100 transacted successfully.

The catch: **withdrawn listings are often excluded from the calculation.** If an agent pulls a property from auction because they expect a poor result, that withdrawal might not count against the clearance rate. This can make the headline number more optimistic than reality.

## What a High Clearance Rate Means for Buyers

In a high-clearance-rate environment (above 70–75%):

- Competition at auction will be fierce
- Vendor expectations are high and vendors rarely negotiate hard before auction
- Off-market strategies become more valuable — finding properties before they reach the competitive auction room

## What a Low Clearance Rate Means for Buyers

When clearance rates soften (below 60%):

- More properties are passed in at auction, creating negotiation opportunities post-auction
- Vendors are more willing to accept conditional offers and private treaty sales
- You have more time to conduct thorough due diligence

## The Local Suburb Rate Is What Matters

National and city-wide clearance rates are interesting but rarely actionable. What matters is the clearance rate in your **target suburb** over the past four to eight weeks. That is the data that tells you whether your competition is increasing or decreasing and whether you need to be more aggressive in your bidding strategy.
